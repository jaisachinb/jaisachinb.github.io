Current Position  ·  May 2026
Predoctoral Researcher in Usable Privacy and Security
Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar  ·  Seeking PhD positions, Fall 2027

My research centers on a single question: how do people make decisions in systems designed to protect or exploit them? Security and privacy technologies succeed or fail at the human level, yet the behavioral, cognitive, and social factors governing how users, defenders, and attackers engage with these systems remain poorly understood.

I approach this through behavioral modeling and human-centered system design, studying attacker cognition, user trust, and decision-making under uncertainty using qualitative methods, structured frameworks such as AMC (Awareness, Motivation, Capability), and large-scale behavioral data. My interdisciplinary work across hardware security, NLP, financial AI, and clinical decision support has made me an amenable researcher, adapting methods across domains while maintaining a consistent focus on the human dimensions of complex systems.

A secondary thread examines how AI mediates human judgment at scale. I treat this as a complement to usable security: understanding how people reason with and against AI is essential to designing systems that are both secure and trustworthy. I am preparing to pursue a PhD at the intersection of HCI and usable security, with an eye toward venues such as CHI, SOUPS, and USENIX Security.
