Incoming Position  ·  May 2026
Research Intern, Usable Privacy and Security
Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar  ·  Survey design and qualitative interview methodology

I work in usable privacy and security — studying why people make the security and privacy decisions they do, and how system design shapes those decisions. Security technologies succeed or fail at the human level, and that is where my attention is directed: at the mental models, habits, and contextual pressures that determine whether users engage meaningfully with protective systems or work around them.

My current work examines how users form and update mental models of security and privacy, how interface and warning design influence security behavior, and how the gap between technical protection and felt user experience becomes an exploitable vulnerability. I work primarily through qualitative methods — surveys and in-depth interviews — grounded in the real experiences of users rather than assumed behavior.

I hold a secondary interest in human-AI interaction, particularly in how AI systems reshape human judgment and trust in security-relevant contexts. As AI becomes embedded in the security decisions of both ordinary users and security professionals, understanding that human side of the system matters.
